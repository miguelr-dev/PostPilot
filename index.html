Insightful · 100–200 words

example
Another week, another copilot announcement. Another demo that wows in the boardroom. Here's the question nobody asks in those meetings: will it survive contact with a real user on a stressed Tuesday morning? The latest wave of AI assistant launches has me thinking about the gap between demos and products. A demo needs to impress once. A product needs to work the thousandth time, for the user who didn't read the docs, in the workflow you didn't anticipate. The teams winning with AI aren't the ones with the fanciest model. They're the ones obsessive about where AI actually removes friction — and honest enough to rip it out when it doesn't move the numbers. Ship one feature that quietly saves thirty seconds. Skip the five that feel like managing a junior intern. Where have you seen AI actually reduce friction — not just add theater? Casual · 100–200 words

example
Everyone keeps asking me when to buy a house, like there's a magic date circled on some calendar. This week's housing report shows inventory finally climbing in most metros. Good news! And also — it changes almost nothing about the real question. Buy when your life needs it and the math works. That's it. Renting isn't throwing money away; it's paying for flexibility. Owning isn't a guaranteed investment; it's a lifestyle choice with a mortgage attached. The thing nobody wants to hear: we massively underbuild housing, then act shocked at prices. Zoning reform would do more for affordability than any first-time-buyer program. Are you waiting on the market, or waiting on your life?
